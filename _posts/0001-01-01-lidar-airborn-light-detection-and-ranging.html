--- title: "LIDAR: Airborn Light Detection and Ranging" image: "https:\/\/i.ytimg.com\/vi\/aN7q9jX6yxs\/hqdefault.jpg" vid_id: "aN7q9jX6yxs" categories: "Science-Technology" tags: ["IRIS","Incorporated Research Institutions for Seismology"] date: "2020-11-17T17:42:27+03:00" vid_date: "2018-04-04T02:47:17Z" duration: "PT2M10S" viewcount: "1029" likeCount: "14" dislikeCount: "0" channel: "IRIS Earthquake Science" --- Airborne LIDAR is an optical remote sensing tool that uses laser pulses to measure the precise distance to the ground. The airplane-mounted laser range-finding system, transmits and receives data, which are precisely located using a highly sensitive GPS receiver. Computer-generated images reveal hidden faults not seen through the vegetation.
This example uses U.S. Geological Survey lidar images taken near Lake Tahoe and reveals a previously unknown fault cross cutting moraines.
